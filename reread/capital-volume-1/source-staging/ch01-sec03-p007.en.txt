The relative form and the equivalent form are inseparable moments of the expression of value, yet at the same time mutually exclusive and opposed poles of that same expression. They are distributed between the different commodities brought into relation. Linen cannot express its value in linen: 20 yards of linen = 20 yards of linen is no expression of value, but only a statement that a definite quantity of the use value linen is identical with itself. The value of linen can therefore be expressed only relatively, in another commodity. This presupposes another commodity standing opposite it in the equivalent form. The commodity that appears as equivalent cannot at the same time take the relative form; it does not express its own value but serves only as the material in which the value of the other commodity is expressed.
