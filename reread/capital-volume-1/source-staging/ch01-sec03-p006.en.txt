Here two different kinds of commodities, A and B—in our example linen and coat—evidently play two different parts. The linen expresses its value in the coat; the coat serves as the material in which that value is expressed. The former plays an active, the latter a passive part. The value of the first commodity is represented as relative value, or it is in the relative form of value. The second commodity functions as equivalent, or is in the equivalent form.
