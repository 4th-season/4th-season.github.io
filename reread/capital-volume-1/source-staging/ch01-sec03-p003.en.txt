We started, in fact, from the exchange-value or exchange-relation of commodities, in order to get at the value concealed beneath it. We must now return to this form under which value first appeared to us. Every one knows, if he knows nothing else, that commodities have a value-form common to them all and standing in marked contrast with the varied bodily forms of their use-values. This is the money-form. We have now to perform a task never even attempted by bourgeois economy: to trace the genesis of this money-form, to develop the expression of value implied in the value-relation of commodities from its simplest and almost imperceptible outline to the dazzling money-form.
