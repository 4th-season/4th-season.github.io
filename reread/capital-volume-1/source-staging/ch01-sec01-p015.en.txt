The magnitude of value of a commodity would therefore remain constant if the labour-time required for its production remained constant. But that labour-time changes with every change in the productivity of labour. Productivity is determined by many circumstances, including the average skill of the workers, the stage of development of science and its technological application, the social organisation of production, the extent and efficiency of the means of production, and natural conditions. The same quantity of labour, for example, is represented by eight bushels of wheat in a favourable season and only four in an unfavourable one; and it yields more metal in rich mines than in poor mines.
